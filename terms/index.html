CharityDrops is a simple service that we’ve endeavored to make easy to understand.

The Terms of Service and Privacy Policy on this page explain the terms you agree to when you use CharityDrops. Simply, by continuing to use the CharityDrops website and service, you agree to be bound by all the terms and conditions set forth below.

Terms of Service

CharityDrops is a website and service to help people discover and support nonprofits.

On CharityDrops, donors signup to donate to our featured nonprofits. Donors also receive email newsletters about the featured nonprofits, which they can, of course, unsubscribe from.

People who aren’t donors can also signup to receive email newsletters about featured nonprofits.

Payments

When you donate through CharityDrops, you agree to donate to the nonprofit beneficiaries we review and select.

CharityDrops collects a small (so we can continue to keep the service alive and make this our full-time position) amount (7.5%) of the total donation amount with a completely optional $1 or more donation to the service (that can be turned on or off in the account management page of any user).

CharityDrops is a service created by IdeaDrops Inc., which is a public benefit entity (means we are legally required to only do work that benefits society and the environment. To make it even simpler: We are legally required to make the world a better place).

The charge on your billing statement will be from CharityDrops.

By signing up as a donor, you authorize CharityDrops to charge your credit card on a 30-day or 31-day cycle. Donors will receive payment confirmation by email with details about their charges.

CharityDrops uses Stripe, Inc. to process payments (third party transaction fees are not a part of CharityDrops’ transaction fee).

Cancellation

You may cancel future donations at any time. If you cancel, donations will be completed to the nonprofit beneficiaries for cycle you’ve last paid. Simply, there are no refunds for cancellation mid-cycle, but you will not be charged again.

We reserve the right to remove any nonprofit beneficiary at any time for any reason without prior notice. In the event that we do remove a nonprofit beneficiary, the donations that would have been given to that nonprofit beneficiary will be designated to another, chosen at our sole discretion. The newly designated nonprofit will, of course, be publicly viewable, and be selected using our publicly viewable selection criteria.

We also reserve the right to cancel anyone’s use of CharityDrops for any reason. Not sure why we’d need to do this, but hey you never know.

Privacy Policy

This policy sets out our privacy and data practices, and explains how we handle and use your data when you use our services. This policy is part of our Terms of Service. By using this website, you consent to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy.

Data Collection

We use Stripe, Inc. to process all donations. We do not store any credit or debit card information ourselves.

We request your email address when you donate, and use it to send you our newsletter. Any other material supplied is completely optional on the Account Management page. Receipts for your donation will come directly from CharityDrops, IdeaDrops Inc., and/or Stripe Inc.,.

We may send other occasional emails to alert you about major news, but will endeavor to keep this minimal. Of course, we also request an email address when anyone signs-up for our newsletter without donating.

We may show donors' names in public “thank you” sections, like a page showing all donors. We may show an anonymized map of donors, without names or personal details.

We don’t collect unnecessary personal data. We won’t transfer your data to third parties other than needed to provide the payments or newsletter services.

We won’t sell your data. We’d never even think of it.

We may use cookies and other web technologies to collect non-personal information about your device, such as your IP address, browser type, and browser language, to improve the quality of our service, and to try and make your browsing experience meaningful.
